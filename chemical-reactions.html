🧪 Chemical Reactions and Equations

Class 10 Chemistry • Chapter 1

📖 Definition of Chemical Reaction

Chemical Reaction

A chemical reaction is a chemical change in which one or more reactants react together to form one or more products having completely different properties.

General Representation:

A + B → C + D

Reactants → Substances that take part in a chemical reaction.

Products → New substances formed after the reaction.

🧪 Activity 1.1

Burning of Magnesium Ribbon

When a clean magnesium ribbon is burnt in air, it reacts with oxygen to form magnesium oxide. A dazzling white flame is produced during the reaction.

2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO

Observation:

  • Magnesium burns with a dazzling white flame.
  • A white powder of magnesium oxide is formed.
  • The product has properties different from magnesium.

🧂 Formation of Precipitate

A precipitate is an insoluble solid formed during a chemical reaction.

Na₂SO₄(aq) + BaCl₂(aq) ↓ BaSO₄(s) + 2NaCl(aq)

🌡️ Change in Temperature

Zn + H₂SO₄ → ZnSO₄ + H₂↑

Zinc reacts with dilute sulphuric acid and heat is produced along with hydrogen gas.

🔥 Exothermic Reaction

An exothermic reaction is a reaction in which heat is released.

Example:

C + O₂ → CO₂ + Heat

❄ Endothermic Reaction

An endothermic reaction is a reaction in which heat is absorbed.

Example:

CaCO₃ + Heat → CaO + CO₂

💧 Change in State

During the burning of a candle, solid wax first melts into liquid wax and finally produces carbon dioxide and water vapour.

🎨 Change in Colour

Potassium permanganate solution is pink in colour. During certain reactions with organic substances, the pink colour gradually disappears, showing that a chemical reaction has taken place.

📝 Chemical Equation

A chemical equation is the shorthand representation of a chemical reaction using chemical symbols and formulae.

Example:

2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO

⚖️ Balanced Chemical Equation

A balanced chemical equation is an equation in which the number of atoms of each element is the same on both the reactant side and the product side.

A balanced chemical equation obeys the Law of Conservation of Mass, which states that mass can neither be created nor destroyed during a chemical reaction.

Balanced equations are generally balanced by the Hit and Trial Method.

Example:

3Fe + 4H₂O → Fe₃O₄ + 4H₂

🧪 Types of Chemical Reactions

1. Combination Reaction

A reaction in which two or more reactants combine to form a single product is called a combination reaction.

Examples:

2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO

C + O₂ → CO₂

2. Decomposition Reaction

A reaction in which a single reactant breaks down into two or more simpler products is called a decomposition reaction.

It takes place in the presence of heat, light or electricity.

Thermal Decomposition

Examples:

2FeSO₄ (Heat) → Fe₂O₃ + SO₂ + SO₃
2Pb(NO₃)₂ (Heat) → 2PbO + 4NO₂ + O₂
CuSO₄·5H₂O (Heat) → CuSO₄ + 5H₂O

The blue copper sulphate crystals become white after losing water of crystallisation.

💨 Evolution of Gas

Zn + H₂SO₄(dil.) → ZnSO₄ + H₂↑

Zinc reacts with dilute sulphuric acid to produce zinc sulphate and hydrogen gas. Heat is also released during the reaction.

📚 Important Definitions

Chemical Reaction

A chemical reaction is a process in which one or more substances are converted into new substances having different properties.

Examples:

  • Cooking of food
  • Digestion of food
  • Respiration
  • Formation of curd

Reactants

The substances that take part in a chemical reaction are called reactants.

Products

The new substances formed after a chemical reaction are called products.

Catalyst

A catalyst is a substance that increases or decreases the rate of a chemical reaction without itself undergoing any permanent chemical change.

📋 Conditions / Indicators of a Chemical Reaction

A chemical reaction is generally identified by one or more of the following observations:

  • 🎨 Change in colour
  • 💨 Evolution of gas
  • 🌡️ Change in temperature
  • 💧 Change in state
  • 🔥 Release of heat (Exothermic reaction)
  • ❄️ Absorption of heat (Endothermic reaction)
  • 🧂 Formation of precipitate

📝 Representing a Chemical Reaction

A chemical reaction can be represented either in words or by using a chemical equation.

Word Equation

Zinc + Sulphuric Acid → Zinc Sulphate + Hydrogen

Chemical Equation

Zn + H₂SO₄ → ZnSO₄ + H₂

📖 Chemical Equation

A chemical equation is the symbolic representation of a chemical reaction using chemical symbols, formulae and physical states of the substances involved.

Example:

3Fe + 4H₂O → Fe₃O₄ + 4H₂

⚖️ Categories of Chemical Equations

1. Balanced Chemical Equation

A balanced chemical equation has an equal number of atoms of each element on both the reactant and product sides.

Example:

3Fe + 4H₂O → Fe₃O₄ + 4H₂

2. Unbalanced (Skeletal) Chemical Equation

An unbalanced chemical equation has an unequal number of atoms on the reactant and product sides.

Example:

Fe + H₂O → Fe₃O₄ + H₂

❓ Why Do We Balance a Chemical Equation?

A chemical equation is balanced to satisfy the Law of Conservation of Mass, which states that mass can neither be created nor destroyed during a chemical reaction.

🧪 Types of Chemical Reactions

  • Combination Reaction
  • Decomposition Reaction
  • Displacement Reaction
  • Double Displacement Reaction
  • Oxidation and Reduction (Redox) Reaction

➕ Combination Reaction

A combination reaction is a reaction in which two or more reactants combine to form a single product.

Examples:

CaO + H₂O → Ca(OH)₂

2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

➖ Decomposition Reaction

A decomposition reaction is a chemical reaction in which a single reactant breaks down into two or more simpler products under suitable conditions such as heat, light or electricity.

Examples:

1. 2FeSO₄ (Heat) → Fe₂O₃ + SO₂ + SO₃

2. CaCO₃ (Heat) → CaO + CO₂

Here,

  • CaCO₃ = Calcium Carbonate (Limestone)
  • CaO = Calcium Oxide (Quicklime)
  • CO₂ = Carbon Dioxide

🔄 Displacement Reaction

A displacement reaction is a chemical reaction in which a more reactive element displaces a less reactive element from its compound.

Example:

Fe + CuSO₄ → FeSO₄ + Cu

🔁 Double Displacement Reaction

A double displacement reaction is a chemical reaction in which the positive and negative ions of two compounds exchange places to form two new compounds.

Example:

Na₂SO₄(aq) + BaCl₂(aq) ↓ BaSO₄(s) + 2NaCl(aq)

🟠 Oxidation Reaction

Oxidation is the addition of oxygen or the removal of hydrogen from a substance.

Examples:

C + O₂ → CO₂

2Cu + O₂ → 2CuO

🔵 Reduction Reaction

Reduction is the addition of hydrogen or the removal of oxygen from a substance.

Example:

CuO + H₂ → Cu + H₂O

⚖️ Redox Reaction

A redox reaction is a reaction in which oxidation and reduction take place simultaneously.

Example:

CuO + H₂ → Cu + H₂O

In this reaction:

  • CuO is reduced because oxygen is removed.
  • H₂ is oxidised because oxygen is added.

🌡️ Reactions Based on Heat Energy

🔥 Exothermic Reaction

An exothermic reaction is a chemical reaction in which heat is released during the formation of products.

Examples:

CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O + Heat

C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Energy

Examples in daily life:

  • Burning of natural gas
  • Respiration

❄️ Endothermic Reaction

An endothermic reaction is a chemical reaction in which heat is absorbed from the surroundings.

Example:

6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Sunlight ↓ C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

Photosynthesis is an example of an endothermic reaction because plants absorb sunlight to prepare food.

⚡ Types of Decomposition Reactions

1. Thermal Decomposition Reaction

A decomposition reaction carried out by heating is called a thermal decomposition reaction.

Examples:

2FeSO₄ (Heat) → Fe₂O₃ + SO₂ + SO₃

CaCO₃ (Heat) → CaO + CO₂

2Pb(NO₃)₂ (Heat) → 2PbO + 4NO₂ + O₂

2. Electrolytic Decomposition Reaction

A decomposition reaction carried out by passing electricity is called an electrolytic decomposition reaction.

Example:

2H₂O → 2H₂ + O₂ (Electricity)

3. Photolytic Decomposition Reaction

A decomposition reaction carried out in the presence of sunlight is called a photolytic decomposition reaction.

Examples:

2AgCl → 2Ag + Cl₂ (Sunlight)

2AgBr → 2Ag + Br₂ (Sunlight)

Silver chloride changes from white to grey when exposed to sunlight. This reaction is used in black and white photography.

🛡️ Corrosion

Corrosion is the slow destruction of metals due to the action of air, moisture, acids or other chemicals present in the environment.

Examples:

  • Rusting of iron
  • Black coating on silver
  • Green coating on copper

Prevention of Corrosion

  • Painting
  • Applying grease or oil
  • Galvanisation (coating iron with zinc)
  • Tin plating or chromium plating
  • Alloying

🥔 Rancidity

Rancidity is the process in which food containing fats and oils gets oxidised, causing unpleasant smell, taste and colour.

Methods to Prevent Rancidity

  • Adding antioxidants
  • Storing food in airtight containers
  • Keeping food in a refrigerator
  • Replacing air with nitrogen gas (chips packets)

🧂 Precipitation Reaction

A precipitation reaction is a chemical reaction in which an insoluble solid (precipitate) is formed.

Example:

Na₂SO₄(aq) + BaCl₂(aq) ↓ BaSO₄(s) + 2NaCl(aq)

A white precipitate of barium sulphate is formed.

❓ Why is Respiration an Exothermic Reaction?

Respiration is an exothermic reaction because glucose is oxidised in our body and releases energy in the form of heat.

Equation:

C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Energy

🏠 White Washing

Step 1:

CaO + H₂O → Ca(OH)₂

Quicklime reacts with water to form slaked lime (calcium hydroxide).

Step 2:

Ca(OH)₂ + CO₂ → CaCO₃ + H₂O

Calcium carbonate gives walls a bright, shiny finish after 2–3 days of whitewashing.
